What is Physics?

Physics is considered one of the most fundamental sciences, forming the basis for understanding the natural world and technological advancements. It is integral to various disciplines, including chemistry, paleontology, and climatology, each of which applies physics to solve specific problems. Physics also underpins all engineering and technology, enabling the development of modern innovations like flat-screen TVs, prosthetics, and efficient tools.

The study of physics is not only intellectually rewarding but also adventurous. It answers everyday mysteries, such as why the sky appears blue or how satellites maintain their orbits.
